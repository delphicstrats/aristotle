# Crisp (now Kroll Crisp)

**Category:** Crisis / Risk Intelligence — Real-Time Reputational Risk Intelligence
**URL:** https://www.kroll.com/en/services/cyber-risk/intelligence-and-investigations
**Scale:** Originally independent; acquired by Kroll (a global risk advisory firm, owned by private equity) in 2023; team of ~100+ specialists; serves Fortune 500 enterprises, financial institutions, and high-profile organizations; positioned at the premium end of reputational risk intelligence.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★★

---

## What It Does

Crisp (operating as Kroll Crisp post-acquisition) was a real-time risk intelligence company specializing in detecting and tracking reputational threats embedded in digital data streams — social media, dark web forums, fringe platforms, and online communities that conventional monitoring tools miss. Its core differentiator was the combination of AI signal detection and human expert analysis: the platform identifies risk signals, and a team of analysts interprets them in context and recommends response strategies.

The acquisition by Kroll positioned Crisp within a broader risk advisory ecosystem: Kroll offers cyber risk, investigative due diligence, and crisis management services. Crisp's reputational risk intelligence became one component of a comprehensive enterprise risk service offering. The combined proposition is intelligence + advisory: not just "here is the threat," but "here is what to do about it and we can help you do it."

Crisp's particular strength was coverage of non-mainstream digital environments — extremist forums, coordinated disinformation campaigns, activist organizing on encrypted or niche platforms. This is early warning intelligence for threats that emerge outside the mainstream media and social media platforms that Dataminr and others monitor.

## SCI Analysis

Crisp/Kroll represents the most human-intelligence-intensive implementation of risk monitoring in the survey. The combination of AI signal detection and expert human analysis is, in effect, a primitive stakeholder intelligence team — analysts who understand not just what signals are appearing, but what they mean in context, what communities are driving them, and what organizational vulnerabilities they target.

This is closer to SCI's posture concept than any purely automated platform: when a Crisp analyst identifies an activist campaign organizing against a company on fringe platforms, they are, in effect, assessing the civil society posture of that stakeholder group. The analysis goes beyond monitoring to interpretation. But it does so on an ad-hoc, reactive basis — triggered by emerging threats rather than structured as an ongoing governance process across a defined stakeholder universe.

The Kroll acquisition reveals the market's recognition that real-time intelligence requires human interpretation to be actionable. This is structurally important for SCI: the governance cycle cannot be fully automated; it requires human judgment at the interpretation and strategy layers. Crisp's model — AI detection + human expertise — is architecturally sound for the intelligence layer. What it lacks is the broader governance framework that would structure how intelligence is gathered, interpreted, and acted upon systematically across all stakeholder domains.

## Competitive Intelligence

Kroll's enterprise client base — financial institutions, major corporations, and organizations facing reputational risk — represents a sophisticated design partner segment. These organizations have already accepted that reputational risk is a governance-level concern requiring professional intelligence infrastructure. The conversation with a Kroll Crisp client about SCI would not need to establish the premise; it would need to demonstrate that proactive stakeholder governance reduces the frequency and severity of the threat situations Crisp is monitoring.

The design partner argument: if organizations governed their stakeholder relationships better across all four domains, fewer threats would emerge requiring Crisp's emergency intelligence services. SCI is the upstream investment that reduces Crisp's downstream utility.

## Notes

The Deloitte "CRiSP" product (Cognitive Risk-Sensing Platform) listed in search results is a separate product with a similar name — Deloitte's own AI-powered digital media risk monitoring tool. Both the Kroll Crisp and Deloitte CRiSP reflect the consulting firm recognition that reputational risk intelligence is a billable service. The pattern — major professional services firms building or acquiring risk intelligence platforms — confirms the enterprise market for this capability.
