# Tractivity

**Category:** Civil Society / Multi-Stakeholder — Stakeholder Engagement Management
**URL:** https://www.tractivity.co.uk
**Scale:** UK-headquartered; serves local government, housing associations, infrastructure developers, and utilities in the UK and internationally; exact revenue not publicly disclosed; positioned as a specialist in UK regulatory stakeholder consultation requirements.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★

---

## What It Does

Tractivity is a stakeholder engagement management system serving UK-focused organizations managing regulated community consultation — local government authorities, housing developers, utilities, transport infrastructure, and similar organizations with legal obligations for public engagement. Its features cover stakeholder contact management, engagement and consultation tracking, public meeting management, issue logging and response tracking, and regulatory compliance reporting.

The platform's UK regulatory context shapes its architecture: UK planning and infrastructure law (the Planning Act, Development Consent Orders, etc.) requires documented consultation with statutory consultees and affected communities. Tractivity is designed to generate the documentation required by these regulatory frameworks — making it, like Borealis, a compliance-driven stakeholder management tool.

## SCI Analysis

Tractivity is comparable to Borealis and Darzin in its functional architecture, with a UK regulatory specialization. The compliance driver is the same: organizations face legal requirements to demonstrate meaningful stakeholder consultation, and Tractivity provides the operational infrastructure to meet those requirements.

From an SCI perspective, Tractivity illustrates a key point: where regulatory requirements for stakeholder engagement exist, the market for stakeholder management software develops rapidly and achieves substantial depth. UK planning law has driven sophisticated stakeholder management tool adoption in a way that market incentives alone have not in other contexts. SCI's argument — that all organizations should govern stakeholder relationships with this level of rigor, regardless of regulatory obligation — is essentially making the case for extending the infrastructure that already exists in regulated contexts to the broader organizational environment.

## Competitive Intelligence

Tractivity's customer base is not a direct design partner target for SCI's initial phase. However, the platform is useful as evidence of the Civil Society domain management market's maturity in regulatory contexts. The UK market specifically has developed sophisticated stakeholder management infrastructure driven by planning law; this regulatory maturation provides useful precedent for what comprehensive stakeholder governance looks like operationally.

## Notes

Tractivity's UK focus means it competes in a more regulated environment than its Antipodean counterparts (Darzin, Simply Stakeholders). The comparison between these markets reveals that regulatory obligation is the primary driver of stakeholder management software adoption — which has implications for SCI's go-to-market: regulatory tailwinds (ESG disclosure, infrastructure permitting, supply chain due diligence) accelerate market adoption of stakeholder governance tools.
