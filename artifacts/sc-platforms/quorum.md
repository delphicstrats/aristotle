# Quorum

**Category:** Government Domain — Public Affairs & Legislative Intelligence
**URL:** https://www.quorum.us
**Scale:** 2,000+ client organizations including 25% of the Fortune 100 (post-Capitol Canary acquisition); Washington, DC-headquartered; raised ~$35M Series B (2021); acquired Capitol Canary (2022); now the dominant full-stack public affairs platform in the US market.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★★★

---

## What It Does

Quorum is the most comprehensive Government Domain platform currently available and the closest structural analog to what SCI proposes — within a single domain. Its architecture unifies legislative tracking and analysis, stakeholder (legislator and staffer) relationship management, grassroots advocacy mobilization (inherited from Capitol Canary), PAC management, coalition building, and impact reporting into one connected workspace. The platform's AI layer ("Quincy") provides legislative summarization, message drafting, semantic search, and policy analysis.

The acquisition of Capitol Canary in 2022 was strategically significant: it added digital grassroots advocacy (mobilizing constituent supporters to contact legislators) to Quorum's existing government intelligence and relationship management capabilities. Together, these capabilities cover the full Government Domain workflow: monitor legislation → identify stakeholders → manage relationships → mobilize advocates → demonstrate impact. This is a genuine governance cycle, implemented for one domain.

Quorum's customer base spans corporations, trade associations, nonprofits, and universities — any organization with a government affairs function. The 1,800+ policy teams and 25% of Fortune 100 penetration reflects genuine market leadership. The platform is purpose-built for government affairs professionals and their specific workflow; it is not a generic CRM adapted for government relations.

## SCI Analysis

Quorum is the strongest existing implementation of SCI's governance cycle concept within any single domain. Its architecture — intelligence gathering, stakeholder mapping, relationship logging, advocacy execution, and impact measurement — maps almost exactly onto the SCI governance cycle as applied to the Government Domain. This is not an accident: government affairs is the most mature and formalized of the four stakeholder domains, with established professional practices that software has successfully codified.

Where Quorum diverges from SCI is scope and philosophical architecture. The platform serves the Government Domain in isolation. A corporation's government affairs team using Quorum has no visibility into — and no structured connection with — the same organization's media relations (Cision), investor relations (Q4), or community engagement (Borealis) functions. Each domain operates independently, with its own tools, workflows, and data. This is precisely the fragmentation that SCI's unified architecture addresses.

Quorum also has no concept of institutional ethos or the say-do gap. It tracks government stakeholder relationships and advocacy effectiveness, but it does not evaluate whether the organization's legislative positions are consistent with its stated values, its ESG commitments, or its public-facing communications. A tobacco company can use Quorum to lobby against FDA regulations while simultaneously running a corporate responsibility campaign — and Quorum's architecture would not register the contradiction. SCI's institutional ethos framework would.

## Competitive Intelligence

Quorum is the most important competitive reference point for SCI's Government Domain component, and also the most instructive case study in what domain-specific governance software looks like when done well. Its success demonstrates that the market for sophisticated, purpose-built stakeholder governance tools is real and significant — the Fortune 100 penetration confirms enterprise willingness to pay for genuine governance infrastructure.

The design partner implication is significant: organizations with mature Quorum implementations are the most likely to understand what a cross-domain governance framework could do for them. A Head of Government Affairs who has seen what structured stakeholder intelligence does for legislative strategy will immediately grasp the value of applying the same rigor to media relations and investor relations. This audience is sophisticated, practiced in the governance vocabulary, and frustrated by the lack of integration across their silos.

## Notes

Quorum's acquisition of Capitol Canary (formerly Phone2Action, a grassroots advocacy platform) is the most important M&A event in the Government Domain in recent years. It brought together the two largest public affairs software platforms and established Quorum's dominance. The combined entity now serves 2,000+ organizations. Quorum CEO Alex Wirth has been explicit about building a "full-service provider" for the public affairs industry — the ambition is to be the complete Government Domain solution.

This also means Quorum is unlikely to expand into other SCI domains. They have staked their identity on Government Domain dominance. SCI's architecture — which begins from cross-domain integration — is a different value proposition, and one that Quorum's existing customer base may be the most qualified to appreciate.
