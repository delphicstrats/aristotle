# Notified (fmr. Intrado Digital Media)

**Category:** Market Domain — IR Communications & Press Distribution
**URL:** https://www.notified.com
**Scale:** Enterprise-focused; rebranded from Intrado Digital Media (2021); owned by West Technology Group (formerly West Corporation); serves public companies and PR teams globally; GlobeNewswire distribution network (key asset) reaches 170+ countries.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★

---

## What It Does

Notified is an integrated communications platform that straddles the Market Domain (investor relations) and Information Domain (press release distribution) — one of the few platforms that attempts meaningful coverage of both. Its architecture combines GlobeNewswire (press release and regulatory filing distribution), IR Cloud (investor relations event management: earnings webcasts, virtual investor days, regulatory filings), media monitoring and social listening, and a media contacts database for PR outreach.

The platform's founding claim is workflow integration: combining PR distribution and IR communications on a single platform, eliminating the vendor fragmentation that forces organizations to use separate tools for GlobeNewswire and their earnings webcasting service. Notified's IR Cloud handles earnings calls, analyst days, and shareholder meetings — the high-stakes moments of investor communications — while its PR platform handles ongoing media relations and distribution.

Notified's customers are primarily enterprise public companies and PR agencies. The GlobeNewswire distribution network is the platform's strongest asset — its reach and credibility with financial media is a genuine competitive differentiator for organizations that need regulatory-grade press release distribution.

## SCI Analysis

Notified is an interesting case because it attempts a limited multi-domain architecture — bridging the Market Domain (IR) and Information Domain (PR/media) — but does so at the functional rather than strategic level. The integration is workflow integration (send your earnings release and your PR release from the same platform), not intelligence integration (understand how your media narrative and your investor narrative interact, and manage them coherently).

From an SCI perspective, Notified's cross-domain gesture is the right impulse improperly implemented. The platform recognizes that IR and PR are not entirely separate — press releases go to both journalists and investors, earnings calls are media events as well as investor events — but it does not develop the architectural implications of that recognition. There is no unified stakeholder model, no posture framework that spans both the Information and Market domains, no governance cycle that coordinates engagement across both.

The say-do gap is invisible here as in other platforms. Notified enables organizations to distribute coordinated messages to journalists and investors simultaneously, which can amplify consistency — or amplify the distance between rhetorical polish and operational reality. The platform would serve a company engaged in regulatory fraud as readily as it would serve a model of institutional integrity.

## Competitive Intelligence

Notified's cross-domain gesture makes it worth noting in design partner conversations as an example of what partial integration looks like — and why it falls short. Organizations using Notified who have experienced the limits of workflow integration (but not strategic integration) are potential design partner candidates. The frustration pattern: "We can send the same release to journalists and investors in one click, but we still can't understand how our media reputation affects our investor relationships."

## Notes

Notified has faced competitive pressure from Q4 (which has taken market share in the IR space) and from Cision's Brandwatch integration (in the PR/monitoring space). The platform's competitive moat is GlobeNewswire's regulatory distribution network — hard to replicate and valuable for compliance-sensitive communications. Ownership under West Technology Group (a private equity-backed telecom and communications services conglomerate) does not suggest aggressive product innovation; Notified is being managed rather than built.
