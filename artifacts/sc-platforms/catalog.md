# Strategic Communications Platform Survey — Master Catalog

*Survey completed: March 2026. Covers 20 platforms and platform categories across all six SCI domain categories.*
*Organized using the SCI stakeholder domain taxonomy.*
*Relevance ratings: ★★★ = closest structural competitor or most directly SCI-relevant; ★★ = relevant to commercial positioning and design partner conversations; ★ = market context.*

---

## Analytical Framing

This survey examines the incumbent software landscape for strategic communications and adjacent disciplines through the lens of the SCI framework. The central analytical question is not "what tools exist?" but "what architectural logic governs each tool, and where does that logic break down?"

The SCI framework proposes that organizations operate in four distinct stakeholder domains — Government, Information, Civil Society, and Market — each conferring a different type of authority over an organization's license to operate. Effective stakeholder governance requires unified intelligence and structured engagement across all four domains, anchored in an honest assessment of institutional ethos (the alignment between stated commitments and observable conduct).

The survey's finding can be stated plainly: **every existing platform is domain-specific, and every existing platform is philosophically shallow about the nature of stakeholder authority.** The market has produced excellent operational tools for managing communications within discrete domains — media relations, government affairs, investor relations, community engagement — but no platform has built the cross-domain architecture that SCI proposes, and no platform has put institutional ethos at the center of its logic.

This is not an oversight. It is a structural consequence of how the market organized itself: each domain developed its own professional disciplines, trade associations, software vendors, and procurement processes. The CCO buys Cision; the government affairs team buys Quorum; the IR team buys Q4; the community relations team buys Borealis. They buy separately, they operate separately, and no one is accountable for the coherence of the whole.

SCI's architecture — and therefore its commercial opportunity — lives in that gap.

## I. Information Domain Platforms

*Media monitoring, media relations, press distribution*

---

### Cision / CisionOne ★★
The dominant incumbent in media intelligence and PR distribution. Serves 75,000+ organizations including 84% of the Fortune 500; ~$826M revenue. CisionOne integrates PR Newswire distribution, journalist database, media monitoring, and social listening. The product of sixteen acquisitions including PR Newswire (2016), Brandwatch (2022), and Trajaan (December 2025). The Brandwatch acquisition represents the most significant attempt to expand beyond traditional PR monitoring into consumer intelligence. Despite its scale, Cision's architecture remains transactional: find a journalist, send a release, track coverage. No posture framework, no governance cycle, no cross-domain architecture. Its customers' frustrations — cost, complexity, and the gap between data volume and strategic insight — define the commercial opportunity immediately above it. → [Full file: cision.md]

### Meltwater ★★
Norwegian-founded media intelligence and social listening platform; ~2,200 employees, 27,000+ clients, taken private by Verdane (2023) at ~$552M. Distinguished from Cision by its emphasis on social data and its new "AI Visibility Tracking" module — the first incumbent platform to explicitly monitor how AI systems (LLMs, search AI) represent brands. This is the most forward-looking product development in the Information Domain: acknowledging that AI has become an epistemic stakeholder in the Information Domain. Still a monitoring-first architecture without governance framework, but the AI Visibility Tracking product warrants a theoretical response from SCI. → [Full file: meltwater.md]

### Muck Rack ★★
The most sophisticated journalist relationship management platform; 300,000+ journalist database; $180M Series A at ~$1.8B valuation (2022). Its journalist-first architecture is, in effect, a primitive posture assessment system: tracking what journalists cover, their positions, and their engagement history. Closest to SCI's posture concept within the Information Domain, but limited to content signals rather than structured authority assessment. Best-in-class for what it does; its customers are among the most relevant design partner candidates for SCI's Information Domain component. → [Full file: muck-rack.md]

### Agility PR Solutions ★
Mid-market all-in-one PR platform; Canadian-headquartered; "PR CoPilot" AI suite. The commoditized middle of the Information Domain — comprehensive workflow coverage at accessible pricing with strong customer service. No architectural depth relevant to SCI. Useful as market context for pricing and mid-market functional expectations. → [Full file: agility-pr.md]

### Prowly ★
SMB-oriented PR management platform acquired by Semrush (2020); 5,000+ customers, pricing from ~$258/month. The most accessible entry point to the Information Domain software market. Defining characteristic: the integrated newsroom. No strategic depth; represents the baseline against which SCI's governance architecture would be a qualitative leap. → [Full file: prowly.md]

### PR Newswire & Business Wire ★
The foundational distribution infrastructure of the Information Domain — the pipes through which corporate statements move. PR Newswire owned by Cision; Business Wire owned by Berkshire Hathaway. Essential compliance infrastructure for publicly traded companies (SEC Regulation FD). No stakeholder intelligence; pure broadcast tools. SCI operates above them, providing the governance intelligence that determines what to say and why. → [Full file: pr-newswire-businesswire.md]

## II. Government Domain Platforms

*Public affairs, government relations, legislative intelligence*

---

### Quorum ★★★
The dominant Government Domain platform and the closest structural analog to what SCI proposes — within a single domain. Post-acquisition of Capitol Canary (2022), Quorum serves 2,000+ organizations including 25% of the Fortune 100 with a full-stack public affairs suite: legislative intelligence, stakeholder relationship management, grassroots advocacy (Capitol Canary), PAC management, coalition building, and impact reporting. Its architecture — intelligence gathering, stakeholder mapping, interaction logging, advocacy execution, measurement — maps almost exactly onto the SCI governance cycle. The most instructive competitive reference point: it demonstrates that the enterprise market will pay for sophisticated, purpose-built stakeholder governance software, and it shows what that software looks like when confined to one domain. Cross-domain integration is the architectural gap Quorum does not fill and shows no interest in filling. → [Full file: quorum.md]

### FiscalNote / PolicyNote ★★
Publicly traded (NYSE: NOTE); raised $1B+ including a SPAC listing (2022) followed by significant financial distress and restructuring. International policy intelligence orientation, CQ congressional intelligence asset, VoterVoice digital advocacy product. Comparable to Quorum in Government Domain capability, stronger internationally, weaker financially. The SPAC-era financial narrative divergence is itself a case study in the say-do gap. VoterVoice's position at the Government-Civil Society intersection is analytically interesting. → [Full file: fiscalnote.md]

### Plural / Capitol Canary (fmr. Phone2Action) ★★ (historical)
Founded 2013; acquired by Quorum in 2022 after serving 1,200+ organizations including 25% of the Fortune 100 as the leading digital advocacy platform. Capitol Canary's grassroots mobilization model operationalized the Government-Civil Society intersection: mobilizing customers, employees, and members (Civil Society actors) to influence legislators (Government Domain targets). Now subsumed into Quorum's suite. Historically significant as evidence of the cross-domain action that organizations already take, even without cross-domain architecture to support it. → [Full file: plural-capitol-canary.md]

## III. Market Domain Platforms

*Investor relations, financial communications*

---

### Q4 Inc. ★★
Publicly traded (TSX: QFOR); 2,600+ public company IR clients; self-described as the leading "IR Ops" platform. The most comprehensive independent investor relations platform: IR website, earnings event management, investor targeting, IR CRM, activist monitoring, and AI tools. Forrester TEI study claims 1,100+ hours annual time savings. Strong governance cycle alignment for the Market Domain — structured, repeatable investor engagement workflow. The architectural absence: no visibility into the Government, Information, or Civil Society factors that directly affect investor confidence. Design partner targets from Q4: companies that have navigated activist campaigns or major ESG controversies and experienced the inadequacy of Market-Domain-only intelligence. → [Full file: q4-inc.md]

### Notified (fmr. Intrado Digital Media) ★
Enterprise communications platform spanning Market Domain (IR Cloud: earnings calls, regulatory filings) and Information Domain (GlobeNewswire distribution, media monitoring). The only incumbent that attempts cross-domain integration between IR and PR — but at the functional workflow level, not the strategic intelligence level. GlobeNewswire distribution is the genuine moat. Currently under competitive pressure from Q4 and Cision; owned by private equity with limited innovation appetite. → [Full file: notified.md]

### Nasdaq IR Insight ★★
Exchange-backed investor relations platform leveraging Nasdaq's proprietary market data for deeper ownership analytics than independent platforms can provide. AI features (2024-2025) include transcript analysis, meeting summarization, and natural language market data querying. Exchange affiliation is the competitive differentiator and the structural constraint: Nasdaq's commercial interests shape the platform's architecture. Strong governance cycle implementation for the Market Domain; no cross-domain awareness. Design partner targets: companies that have faced multi-stakeholder pressure (activist campaigns, ESG controversies) where Market Domain intelligence alone proved insufficient. → [Full file: nasdaq-ir-insight.md]

### Irwin ★
Growth-stage IR CRM specialist (raised C$20M Series B, 2022); FactSet integration for premium financial data. The Muck Rack of investor relations: deep relationship management within the Market Domain, with investor interaction logging, meeting prep tools, and board reporting. Domain-specialist platforms competing on depth rather than breadth. Secondary design partner priority. → [Full file: irwin.md]

## IV. Civil Society / Multi-Stakeholder Platforms

*Stakeholder mapping and engagement management*

---

### Borealis ★★★
The most sophisticated Civil Society domain platform in the survey; serves infrastructure, energy, mining, and utilities sectors with a regulatory compliance driver. Architecture: stakeholder database, interaction logging, commitment tracking, GIS mapping, and regulatory compliance reporting. The commitment tracking feature is SCI's say-do gap operationalized as compliance requirement — organizations must log what they promised stakeholders and demonstrate follow-through. The GIS component treats geography as a governance variable. Cross-domain architecture is absent but the Civil Society governance cycle is implemented more completely here than anywhere else in the market. Regulatory tailwinds (ESG disclosure requirements, infrastructure permitting) are building this market category. Design partner targets: large infrastructure companies managing community engagement who already use Borealis and understand structured stakeholder governance. → [Full file: borealis.md]

### Darzin ★
Australian-headquartered stakeholder CRM for government agencies, infrastructure projects, and community engagement programs. "Like a CRM, but specifically for managing stakeholders." Accessible, functional, and analytically limited. Native survey and polling tools distinguish it from some competitors. Civil Society domain operational layer without strategic depth. → [Full file: darzin.md]

### Simply Stakeholders ★
Cloud-based stakeholder relationship management system for education, government, and infrastructure contexts; positioned as the accessible Borealis alternative. User-friendly implementation of Civil Society domain stakeholder tracking. Market validation of the stakeholder CRM concept; no architectural relevance to SCI beyond confirming market demand. → [Full file: simply-stakeholders.md]

### Jambo ★
Canadian stakeholder management platform purpose-built for government teams managing public engagement. Interesting architecture inversion: serves government agencies as Civil Society engagement managers rather than serving organizations managing government stakeholders. → [Full file: jambo.md]

### Tractivity ★
UK-headquartered stakeholder engagement management platform; regulatory compliance driver from UK planning law (Development Consent Orders, infrastructure permitting). UK regulatory context has produced sophisticated stakeholder management tool adoption. Confirms that regulatory obligation is the primary driver of Civil Society domain software adoption — relevant for SCI's regulatory tailwind thesis. → [Full file: tractivity.md]

## V. Reputation Intelligence

*Cross-domain monitoring and reputation scoring*

---

### RepTrak ★★★
The world's leading reputation measurement and intelligence platform; 250+ enterprise clients in 30+ industries; methodology based on seven reputation dimensions measured through stakeholder surveys. The December 2025 launch of "RepTrak Compass" (always-on reputation data, AI advisory) represents genuine product ambition. The "AI as a Stakeholder" product — tracking how LLMs and AI search systems represent companies — is the most intellectually significant product development in the platform landscape. Most philosophically proximate to SCI's architecture of any platform in the survey: the RepTrak model implicitly measures institutional character, and its advisory services approximate stakeholder governance guidance. The gap: population-level perception measurement is not relationship-level stakeholder governance. No engagement architecture, no CRM, no governance cycle. RepTrak's enterprise customers are the highest-value design partner segment in the survey. → [Full file: reptrak.md]

### Brandwatch ★★
Acquired by Cision (2022) for ~$450M; 1.4 trillion conversation archive; 100M+ source coverage. Enterprise social listening and consumer intelligence. The Cision acquisition integrates Brandwatch's social data with Cision's media database for combined earned media + social intelligence. Sophisticated analytical capabilities; architecturally still monitoring-first. Cross-domain signal incidence (Civil Society and Information Domain signals appearing in same data stream) is incidental, not architectural. → [Full file: brandwatch.md]

### Signal AI ★★★
London-headquartered AI-powered reputation and risk intelligence platform; raised $50M+; covers 226 markets and 75 languages. The most explicitly upmarket positioning of any monitoring platform — targeting "strategic communicators" at the CCO level, not PR practitioners. Semantic AI analysis (vs. keyword monitoring) provides genuine analytical depth. Touches Government Domain (regulatory monitoring), Information Domain (media intelligence), and Civil Society Domain (NGO and advocacy narrative tracking) through its signal detection — the closest any monitoring platform comes to SCI's multi-domain architecture. Gap: delivers signals without a stakeholder governance framework for systematic response. Design partner targets: CCOs and communications directors at global enterprises who use Signal AI's intelligence but manually synthesize cross-domain stakeholder analysis. → [Full file: signal-ai.md]

### Talkwalker (acquired by Hootsuite, April 2024) ★★
Luxembourg-founded AI-powered social listening and consumer intelligence platform; 2,500+ enterprise clients pre-acquisition; acquired by Hootsuite for integration into social media management workflow. The acquisition illustrates the commoditization of social listening: premium enterprise intelligence capability absorbed into social media management infrastructure. Confirms that the social listening layer is commoditizing, clearing space for the next intelligence layer — stakeholder governance. → [Full file: talkwalker.md]

### Sprinklr ★★★
Publicly traded (NYSE: CXM); ~$800M ARR; 1,400+ enterprise customers; the most ambitious integration attempt in the communications platform landscape. "Unified Customer Experience Management" (Unified-CXM) covers contact center, social listening, marketing, and social media management across 30+ channels. The most instructive negative case study for SCI: Sprinklr achieves genuine cross-functional integration at the customer relationship layer, not the stakeholder governance layer. Its stakeholders are customers and digital audiences, not the Government/Information/Civil Society/Market authority holders that SCI addresses. Demonstrates that integration must happen at the right architectural level, not just achieve any integration. → [Full file: sprinklr.md]

## VI. Crisis / Risk Intelligence

*Rapid signal detection and response*

---

### Onclusive ★★
Formed from merger of AirPR and Kantar Media Monitoring elements (2021); 4,000+ clients; the most analytically rigorous PR measurement platform. Emphasis on connecting earned media coverage to business outcomes — the most serious attempt to solve the ROI measurement problem in the Information Domain. Architecture: monitoring + measurement, not monitoring + governance. Measures what happened; does not govern what should happen. Design partner targets: VP/Director of Communications Analytics who has built measurement infrastructure and is being asked to make it strategic. → [Full file: onclusive.md]

### Dataminr ★★
New York-headquartered real-time event and threat intelligence platform; raised $900M+ (Series F at $4.1B valuation, 2021); serves financial institutions, global enterprises, government agencies, and news organizations. The fastest signal detection system in the commercial market — earliest warning of emerging events from social and digital data streams. 2025 agentic AI roadmap moves from alert delivery toward automated response orchestration. SCI's early warning system at the real-time layer. Gap: delivers signals without stakeholder governance framework for interpretation and systematic response. Design partner targets: organizations with mature Dataminr implementations and sophisticated CCO-CRO relationship, frustrated by lack of integration between risk intelligence and communications governance. → [Full file: dataminr.md]

### Crisp (now Kroll Crisp) ★★
Originally independent real-time reputational risk intelligence company; acquired by Kroll (2023), a global risk advisory firm. Specializes in detecting threats in non-mainstream digital environments — extremist forums, coordinated disinformation campaigns, activist organizing on encrypted or niche platforms. Combines AI signal detection with human expert analysis — the most human-intelligence-intensive monitoring implementation in the survey. The Kroll acquisition model (intelligence + advisory) mirrors SCI's intelligence + governance architecture at the crisis intervention layer. Gap: reactive and domain-unspecific; SCI's proactive governance cycle reduces the frequency of situations requiring Crisp-level emergency intelligence. → [Full file: crisp-kroll.md]

## VII. Integrated Attempts

*Platforms making a multi-domain play*

---

### Edelman CommsTech Solutions ★★★
Proprietary intelligence platform embedded within Edelman advisory services; not independently sold. The most philosophically proximate attempt at multi-domain stakeholder intelligence from the world's largest PR firm. Edelman's Trust Barometer data architecture — tracking trust across Government, Business, NGOs, and Media for 25 years — is a population-level implementation of SCI's four-domain model. CommsTech promises to operationalize that at the account level. Structural limitations: bundled with advisory (no client ownership), primarily Information Domain and reputation orientation, institutional ethos blind spot (commercial incentives constrain honest say-do assessment). Edelman's senior alumni represent a design partner discovery pathway. → [Full file: edelman-commstech.md]

### Salesforce (Generic CRM — Contextual) ★
$35B+ annual revenue; the world's dominant CRM. Not a stakeholder governance platform, but the de facto fallback for organizations that lack purpose-built tools — government affairs teams, IR teams, and communications teams building customized Salesforce configurations to approximate stakeholder management. The widespread misapplication of sales CRM architecture to stakeholder governance is itself evidence of the architectural gap that SCI fills: the need is real, the existing tools are wrong, and organizations are making do. 2025's Agentforce (agentic AI) may produce a new wave of sophisticated custom Salesforce implementations — or may accelerate the recognition that purpose-built governance architecture is more valuable. → [Full file: salesforce-generic-crm.md]

## Competitive Landscape Summary

The fragmented landscape tells a coherent story about where SCI fits — and why no incumbent can credibly claim to fill that space.

**The domain trap.** Every dedicated strategic communications platform is built within and for a single stakeholder domain. Cision owns the Information Domain. Quorum owns the Government Domain. Q4 owns the Market Domain. Borealis leads the Civil Society Domain. Each is excellent within its walls. None can see over them. This is not a product failure; it is an organizational and market failure. The professional disciplines — PR, government affairs, IR, community relations — developed independently with separate training, separate associations, and separate procurement budgets. Software vendors followed the buyers.

**The monitoring addiction.** Even the most sophisticated cross-domain platforms (Signal AI, RepTrak) are monitoring-first architectures: they tell you what is happening, not how to govern it. The market has produced excellent observation infrastructure and poor governance infrastructure. SCI's architecture puts governance — structured, repeatable stakeholder engagement with defined accountability — at the center, with intelligence as an input, not an output.

**The ethos evasion.** No platform in the survey directly addresses the say-do gap — the gap between what organizations say and what they do. This is not accidental. Monitoring platforms are paid to report what is being said about organizations. Advisory firms are paid to improve what organizations say. Neither has a commercial interest in evaluating whether what organizations say is true. SCI's institutional ethos concept — which puts say-do alignment at the center of stakeholder governance — is philosophically uncomfortable for every existing commercial model in this market.

**The measurement-governance gap.** RepTrak can tell you your reputation score. Onclusive can tell you how much coverage you got. Dataminr can alert you when something is happening. What the market cannot give you is a structured governance cycle: a repeatable process for defining stakeholder posture, determining engagement strategy, executing engagement, evaluating outcomes, and updating the posture assessment. The governance cycle — the organizational translation of kairos — is the missing architectural layer.

**The integration ceiling.** The most ambitious integration attempt in the market (Sprinklr) integrates at the wrong level: customer communication channels rather than stakeholder authority domains. The most philosophically aligned attempt (Edelman CommsTech) is bundled with advisory services in a model that denies clients ownership. The market has not produced — and no incumbent appears positioned to produce — a platform that integrates across the four stakeholder domains at the governance level.

This is SCI's territory. The gap is real, the market is real, and no one is standing in it.

## Design Partner Implications

The landscape points to specific design partner profiles — organizations sophisticated enough to recognize the gap and motivated to fill it.

**Highest-priority segment: RepTrak enterprise customers.** Organizations that have already invested in reputation as a board-level governance challenge. They have accepted the intellectual premise, demonstrated willingness to pay for measurement and advisory services, and are positioned to recognize that measurement without governance architecture is incomplete. The CCO or Chief Reputation Officer at a global enterprise using RepTrak for board reporting but frustrated by the gap between scores and governance strategy is the ideal SCI design partner.

**High-priority segment: Quorum power users at large corporations.** Organizations with mature Government Domain governance programs who feel the absence of cross-domain integration acutely. A Head of Government Affairs who has seen what structured stakeholder intelligence does for legislative strategy immediately understands the value of applying the same rigor to media relations, investor relations, and community engagement. The sophistication is there; the cross-domain architecture is not.

**High-priority segment: Large infrastructure companies using Borealis.** Organizations that have already implemented formal Civil Society stakeholder governance as an operational requirement — permitting, regulatory compliance, social license. These organizations have operationalized the governance cycle within one domain and can immediately grasp the value of extending it to the others. The ESG disclosure regulatory tailwind (EU CSRD, SEC climate rules) is building urgency.

**High-priority segment: Signal AI CCO-level users.** Senior communications leaders at global enterprises who use Signal AI's multi-domain intelligence but manually synthesize cross-domain stakeholder analysis in PowerPoints. They already think across domains; they lack the architecture to govern across domains.

**Secondary segment: Q4 and Nasdaq IR Insight clients who have navigated activist campaigns or major ESG controversies.** These are organizations that experienced the inadequacy of Market Domain intelligence in isolation — that investor confidence depends on government, media, and civil society dynamics that IR platforms cannot see. Post-crisis receptivity to cross-domain governance architecture is high.

**The advisory firm angle.** Edelman alumni, Kekst CNC alumni, and Brunswick Group practitioners who have thought seriously about multi-domain stakeholder intelligence are both design partner discovery pathways and potential design partners themselves if they are now in-house at major organizations. These practitioners already understand the governance vocabulary and the domain taxonomy; they are waiting for software that matches their sophistication.

**What to avoid in the early phase.** Mid-market organizations using Agility PR, Prowly, or generic Salesforce configurations are not design partner targets — they have not yet formalized stakeholder governance as a strategic function. The conversation needs to start with organizations that have already accepted the governance premise; SCI's first task is architecture, not evangelism.
