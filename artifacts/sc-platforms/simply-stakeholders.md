# Simply Stakeholders

**Category:** Civil Society / Multi-Stakeholder — Stakeholder Engagement Management
**URL:** https://simplystakeholders.com
**Scale:** Australian and international; cloud-based; serves education institutions, government agencies, infrastructure projects, and NGOs; mid-market focus; exact revenue not publicly disclosed; positions itself as the most user-friendly alternative to Borealis.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★

---

## What It Does

Simply Stakeholders is a cloud-based stakeholder relationship management system that emphasizes ease of use and implementation speed as its primary differentiators. Its core features include stakeholder database management, interaction and engagement tracking, issues and sentiment logging, public consultation management (for government and infrastructure contexts), and reporting dashboards. The platform serves organizations managing structured community consultation, public participation processes, or multi-party stakeholder programs.

The platform's self-positioning against Borealis — explicitly marketed as a Borealis alternative — reveals the competitive dynamics of the Civil Society domain software market: organizations comparing these tools are looking for a balance between feature depth and operational simplicity. Simply Stakeholders positions itself as easier to implement and use than Borealis while covering the essential stakeholder engagement workflow.

## SCI Analysis

Simply Stakeholders occupies the user-friendly end of the Civil Society domain management market — functional, accessible, and adequate for organizations managing consultation requirements without needing the full depth of enterprise platforms like Borealis. From an SCI perspective, it represents the minimum viable stakeholder governance infrastructure: you can track who you engaged, when, and what they said, and generate reports demonstrating that engagement happened.

This is necessary but far from sufficient for SCI's governance architecture. The platform provides no posture framework, no domain taxonomy, no cross-domain awareness, and no institutional ethos alignment checking. It is a structured contact management and interaction logging tool for Civil Society relationships — valuable in its domain, invisible beyond it.

## Competitive Intelligence

Simply Stakeholders' customer base — educational institutions, government agencies, infrastructure project teams — is not the primary design partner segment for SCI. However, its position in the market confirms the growing recognition across sectors that stakeholder engagement requires purpose-built tools. The expansion of stakeholder management software into education and government contexts suggests broadening awareness of the Civil Society domain as a formal governance function.

## Notes

The comparison market (Borealis vs. Simply Stakeholders vs. Darzin vs. Jambo vs. Tractivity) reveals a commoditizing Civil Society domain tool market: multiple platforms offering similar core features at similar price points, differentiating on usability, customer support, and vertical specialization. This commoditization at the operational layer confirms that the strategic differentiation opportunity lies above the CRM layer — in the governance and intelligence frameworks that give the operational data meaning. SCI's architecture lives at that higher layer.
