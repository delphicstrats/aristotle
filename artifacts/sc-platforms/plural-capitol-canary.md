# Plural / Capitol Canary (fmr. Phone2Action)

**Category:** Government Domain — Digital Advocacy & Grassroots Mobilization
**URL:** Acquired by Quorum (2022); formerly capitolcanary.com
**Scale:** At acquisition, served 1,200+ organizations including 25% of the Fortune 100; Arlington, VA-headquartered; founded as Phone2Action (2013), rebranded to Capitol Canary before Quorum acquisition in 2022; now integrated into Quorum's product suite.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★★ (historical; now subsumed into Quorum)

---

## What It Does

Capitol Canary (operating under the Plural brand post-acquisition context) was the leading digital advocacy and grassroots mobilization platform — software that enables organizations (corporations, associations, nonprofits) to mobilize their employees, members, or customers to contact legislators and regulators on policy issues. Its core workflow: organizations create advocacy campaigns, identify their advocate base, enable advocates to send messages to their specific legislators, and track the resulting engagement and impact.

The platform was built on the insight that legislative influence is not only about direct lobbying — it is also about demonstrating constituent support. When 50,000 customers of a major retailer contact their congressperson about a trade policy, that grassroots signal carries different weight than a lobbyist meeting. Capitol Canary's technology enabled organizations to mobilize that constituent signal at scale.

Following Quorum's 2022 acquisition, Capitol Canary's capabilities were integrated into Quorum's public affairs suite. The combined entity now offers the full Government Domain stack: legislative intelligence + relationship management + grassroots advocacy + PAC management + impact reporting.

## SCI Analysis

Capitol Canary's digital advocacy model is the Government Domain's equivalent of what RepTrak does in the Civil Society domain — mobilizing a constituency to demonstrate normative legitimacy to decision-makers. The implicit theory of influence is exactly what SCI's Government Domain framework would predict: legislators respond not just to direct organizational lobbying but to the aggregated pressure of their constituents, and organizations with large constituent networks can translate that into policy influence.

What makes Capitol Canary particularly interesting from an SCI perspective is its position at the intersection of the Government and Civil Society domains: digital advocacy mobilizes individuals (Civil Society actors: employees, customers, members) to influence government decision-makers. This is a cross-domain action — using Civil Society networks to shape Government Domain outcomes — but it is implemented without any explicit cross-domain architecture. The platform facilitates the action without theorizing the stakeholder dynamics that make it work.

The acquisition by Quorum eliminated the independent platform, but the underlying capability — grassroots advocacy at scale — is now the most sophisticated element of Quorum's Government Domain suite.

## Competitive Intelligence

The Capitol Canary acquisition is instructive for SCI's design partner search: organizations that used Capitol Canary independently (before it was subsumed into Quorum) are organizations that understood the multi-stakeholder dimension of government affairs — that government influence requires mobilizing Civil Society networks, not just deploying lobbyists. These organizations are already thinking at the intersection of Government and Civil Society domains. They are natural audiences for SCI's cross-domain governance framework.

## Notes

The Phone2Action → Capitol Canary → Quorum trajectory reflects the consolidation pressure in the Government Domain software market. As Quorum absorbs more capabilities, the market for independent point solutions in government affairs technology narrows. This consolidation creates both opportunity and risk for SCI: opportunity in that the remaining independent players are looking for differentiation; risk in that Quorum's comprehensive Government Domain suite may satisfy enough of the market need that the urgency for cross-domain integration feels less acute.
