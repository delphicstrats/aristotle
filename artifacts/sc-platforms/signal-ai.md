# Signal AI

**Category:** Reputation Intelligence — AI-Powered Media & Risk Intelligence
**URL:** https://signal-ai.com
**Scale:** London-headquartered; raised ~$50M+ in funding (including a $25M Series C in 2021); ~200 employees; enterprise-focused; covers 226 markets and 75 languages; serves strategic communications and corporate affairs leaders.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★★★

---

## What It Does

Signal AI is a reputation and risk intelligence platform built on proprietary AI — moving beyond Boolean keyword search to semantic AI-powered analysis of external data from traditional media, social media, regulatory filings, and other digital sources. The platform explicitly targets "strategic communicators" rather than PR practitioners: its self-positioning is about enterprise-level reputation intelligence for CCO-level functions, not media monitoring for PR teams.

Signal AI's core capabilities include reputation tracking across 226 markets, regulatory and risk intelligence (monitoring for signals that may affect corporate risk), competitor intelligence, crisis early warning, and media monitoring across print, broadcast, online, and social. The semantic AI approach allows for more nuanced signal detection than keyword monitoring — identifying contextual patterns rather than just literal mentions.

What distinguishes Signal AI in the landscape is its explicit upmarket positioning: it presents itself as the intelligence layer for senior communications leaders, not a workflow tool for PR practitioners. The website language emphasizes "actionable insights," "strategic communicators," and "enterprise risk" — territory that begins to approximate SCI's governance architecture, even if the platform itself does not deliver governance capability.

## SCI Analysis

Signal AI is the platform that most consciously aspires to the SCI domain — a reputation intelligence tool for strategic communicators thinking at the enterprise governance level — without achieving the architectural depth SCI implies. The semantic AI approach is genuinely more sophisticated than keyword monitoring: detecting patterns, identifying emerging narratives, and surfacing signals before they become crises represents a real advance over Cision and Meltwater's monitoring architectures.

But Signal AI's intelligence is still fundamentally observational. It surfaces signals from the external environment without providing the stakeholder governance framework to act on those signals systematically. The platform knows that a regulatory inquiry is emerging in a specific market; it does not help the organization understand what that means for their relationship with the relevant regulatory authority, what posture that authority holds toward them, or what governance response the situation requires. Intelligence without governance architecture is surveillance without strategy.

Signal AI touches the Government Domain (through regulatory monitoring), the Information Domain (media intelligence), and implicitly the Civil Society Domain (tracking NGO and advocacy narratives). This cross-domain signal detection is the closest any monitoring platform comes to SCI's multi-domain architecture. The gap is that Signal AI delivers signals from multiple domains without a unified stakeholder relationship model to interpret and act on them.

## Competitive Intelligence

Signal AI's explicit targeting of senior communications leaders makes its customer base one of the most relevant design partner segments in the survey. CCOs and communications directors at global enterprises using Signal AI are already invested in the premise that strategic reputation intelligence should operate at a different level than PR monitoring. The leap from Signal AI's intelligence layer to SCI's governance architecture is conceptually achievable for this audience — they already think about stakeholders across domains; they just lack the framework to govern those relationships systematically.

The design partner from Signal AI's customer base: a CCO or Chief of Staff for Communications at a global enterprise who uses Signal AI's intelligence but finds themselves manually synthesizing cross-domain stakeholder intelligence in PowerPoints for the board — precisely because no platform gives them an integrated governance view.

## Notes

Signal AI's geographic emphasis — 226 markets, 75 languages — reflects its origins as a global enterprise intelligence tool, serving multinationals for whom regional reputation dynamics are material. This international scope is a competitive differentiator relative to US-centric platforms like Quorum or Q4. For SCI, the international dimension of stakeholder governance — how Government, Information, Market, and Civil Society domains operate differently across jurisdictions — is a significant architectural consideration that Signal AI's data infrastructure could inform.
