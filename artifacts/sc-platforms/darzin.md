# Darzin

**Category:** Civil Society / Multi-Stakeholder — Stakeholder CRM
**URL:** https://www.darzin.com
**Scale:** Australian-headquartered; SMB to mid-market focused; subscription-based SaaS; serves government agencies, infrastructure projects, and community engagement programs; exact revenue not publicly disclosed; positioned as the accessible alternative to enterprise stakeholder management platforms.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★

---

## What It Does

Darzin is a stakeholder CRM — explicitly positioned as "like a CRM, but specifically for managing stakeholders." Its architecture provides stakeholder contact management and segmentation, interaction logging across the whole team, issue and sentiment tracking, native survey and polling tools, and reporting dashboards. The platform targets any organization that needs to manage complex multi-party stakeholder relationships but finds sales CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot) inadequate for the purpose.

Darzin's key claim is simplicity: it is designed for stakeholder engagement managers, community relations teams, and government affairs teams who need a functional tool without enterprise complexity. The native survey and polling features distinguish it from some competitors who rely on external integrations for public consultation data collection.

Darzin's customers include government agencies running public consultation processes, infrastructure project teams managing community relations, and nonprofit organizations managing donor and partner relationships. The Australian origin reflects the regulatory environment for community consultation in extractive industries (mining, energy) which has driven significant stakeholder management software adoption in that region.

## SCI Analysis

Darzin is a functional implementation of Civil Society domain stakeholder management at the operational level. Its CRM architecture captures the essential logic: stakeholders are discrete entities with histories of interaction, positions on issues, and relationships to each other and to the organization. Mapping and tracking those relationships is the foundational layer of any stakeholder governance system.

But Darzin is operationally functional without being strategically sophisticated. The platform provides a shared record of stakeholder interactions; it does not provide a framework for assessing stakeholder posture, structuring governance strategy, or connecting Civil Society engagement to an organization's broader stakeholder governance architecture. The tool records what happened; SCI's framework provides the analytical structure for deciding what should happen and evaluating whether it achieved the intended outcome.

## Competitive Intelligence

Darzin's customers are not the primary design partner targets for SCI — they are typically government agencies and project teams who need operational coordination tools, not strategic governance frameworks. However, Darzin reveals something important about the Civil Society domain: the market has validated the stakeholder CRM concept broadly, across organization types and geographies. The existence of multiple dedicated stakeholder CRM platforms (Darzin, Simply Stakeholders, Jambo, Tractivity, Borealis) confirms that the market recognizes Civil Society stakeholder management as a distinct discipline requiring purpose-built tools.
