# Jambo

**Category:** Civil Society / Multi-Stakeholder — Stakeholder Management for Government
**URL:** https://blog.jambo.cloud
**Scale:** Canadian-headquartered; government and infrastructure sector focus; SMB to mid-market; exact revenue not publicly disclosed; positions itself as the stakeholder management platform purpose-built for government teams managing public engagement.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★

---

## What It Does

Jambo is a stakeholder management platform explicitly designed for government teams and infrastructure project managers who need to manage high volumes of stakeholder data and public engagement records. Its core features include stakeholder database management, engagement and interaction logging, reporting on engagement outcomes, and tools for meeting and fostering meaningful engagement with community stakeholders. The platform emphasizes public sector usability: designed for government staff who manage community consultations, public hearings, and regulatory engagement processes.

Jambo's comparison positioning against Simply Stakeholders focuses on its government team orientation — claiming superior handling of high-volume stakeholder data in complex government contexts, and better reporting for demonstrating engagement outcomes to regulatory bodies and the public.

## SCI Analysis

Jambo is functionally similar to Darzin and Simply Stakeholders — a Civil Society domain stakeholder CRM with government sector specialization. From an SCI perspective, its government team focus is analytically interesting: government agencies themselves are Civil Society actors when they engage with communities, and Government Domain actors when they regulate other organizations. Jambo's customers are on the providing end of the Government-Civil Society relationship that the SCI framework addresses from the organizational side.

This is a useful perspective inversion: while SCI is designed for organizations managing their relationships with government stakeholders, Jambo serves the government teams managing their relationships with community stakeholders. The same underlying logic applies — structured stakeholder intelligence, interaction logging, posture assessment, governance cycle — but from the other side of the relationship.

## Competitive Intelligence

Jambo's government team customer base is not a direct design partner target for SCI, which is primarily aimed at organizations managing stakeholder relationships externally. However, the platform illustrates the breadth of the Civil Society domain management market and confirms that stakeholder governance software is recognized across both public and private sector organizations as a necessary tool.
