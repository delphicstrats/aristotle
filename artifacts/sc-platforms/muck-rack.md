# Muck Rack

**Category:** Information Domain — PR Management & Media Relations
**URL:** https://muckrack.com
**Scale:** ~$100M+ ARR (estimated); raised $180M Series A in 2022 (Susquehanna Growth Equity); database of 300,000+ journalists and media contacts; primarily US enterprise and agency market.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★★

---

## What It Does

Muck Rack is a PR management platform built around a core insight: the fundamental relationship in media relations is between the communications professional and the journalist, not between the organization and the media outlet. Its product centers on a constantly updated journalist database — tracking journalist beats, social media activity, article history, and contact preferences — combined with outreach management, coverage monitoring, and reporting tools. The platform prices in the $15,000+/year range and has carved out significant enterprise and agency market share.

What distinguishes Muck Rack from Cision and Meltwater is its explicit journalist-first orientation. The platform tracks journalist behavior and signals (what they're writing about, what they're tweeting, what they've recently covered) to help PR professionals pitch more relevantly and build genuine relationships. This is not merely a database — it is an attempt to make journalist relationship management intelligent. The platform's reporting tools also help PR teams demonstrate the value of coverage to internal stakeholders, addressing the measurement problem that plagues communications functions.

## SCI Analysis

Muck Rack is the most sophisticated implementation of Information Domain stakeholder management among the pure-PR platforms. Its journalist-first architecture is, in effect, a primitive posture assessment system: by tracking what a journalist covers, their recent positions, and their engagement history, the platform helps PR professionals understand where a journalist sits relative to their organization's story. This is directionally aligned with SCI's posture concept, though philosophically underdeveloped.

The limitation is scope and depth. Muck Rack reduces posture to content signals — what a journalist has recently covered — rather than a structured assessment of the journalist's relationship to the organization, their authority within their domain, and the historical pattern of engagement. It also has no framework for the governance cycle — no structured process for deciding who to engage, when, at what level, and what outcome to target. The relationship management tools are engagement logging rather than governance architecture.

Like all Information Domain platforms, Muck Rack has no concept of the other three SCI domains. A pharmaceutical company's government affairs engagement, investor relations strategy, and community stakeholder work are entirely invisible in Muck Rack. The communications professional using Muck Rack is managing one relationship type in isolation.

## Competitive Intelligence

Muck Rack's customer profile — enterprise PR teams and agencies who take journalist relationship quality seriously — represents a highly relevant design partner segment for SCI. These are professionals who already understand that stakeholder posture matters; they just have inadequate tools for structuring and scaling that understanding. The leap from Muck Rack's implicit posture signals to SCI's explicit posture framework is conceptually manageable for this audience.

Agency customers are particularly interesting: large PR agencies managing multiple client stakeholder ecosystems are structurally underserved by single-client-centric tools. A multi-domain stakeholder governance platform that could serve agencies managing government affairs, media relations, and community engagement across multiple clients would represent a significant architectural upgrade.

## Notes

Muck Rack's 2022 Series A ($180M at a reported $1.8B+ valuation) reflects genuine market confidence in the PR management software space. The company has been profitable and growing without previous institutional capital — the raise was to accelerate product and market expansion. Leadership is focused on enterprise; the AI features (AI-powered journalist matching, pitch optimization) represent the current development frontier. Watch for whether Muck Rack attempts cross-domain expansion or remains focused on deepening its Information Domain specialization.
