# FiscalNote (PolicyNote)

**Category:** Government Domain — Policy Intelligence & Government Affairs
**URL:** https://fiscalnote.com
**Scale:** Publicly traded (NYSE: NOTE); raised $1B+ across funding rounds; went public via SPAC merger (2022); 2023 revenue ~$130M; significant financial distress reported post-IPO with restructuring efforts; flagship product rebranded from FiscalNote to PolicyNote.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★★

---

## What It Does

FiscalNote was founded in 2013 on the premise that AI could make legislative intelligence accessible at scale — tracking bills, amendments, regulations, and executive orders across federal, state, and international jurisdictions, and delivering that intelligence through an integrated workflow platform. The platform's current flagship, PolicyNote, covers federal and state legislative tracking, stakeholder relationship management for legislators and staffers, international policy monitoring, digital advocacy (via VoterVoice, acquired), and congressional news analysis (via CQ, also acquired).

The platform's ambition was explicitly broader than Quorum: FiscalNote positioned itself as a global policy intelligence company, covering international regulatory environments and serving multinational corporations. The AI components handle legislative summarization, policy brief generation, stakeholder mapping, and impact analysis. The company pursued an aggressive acquisition strategy — CQ (2021), VoterVoice (2016, providing digital advocacy), and others — to build a comprehensive suite.

The SPAC-fueled public listing revealed significant financial problems: post-merger losses, covenant issues, and a restructuring that included layoffs and asset divestitures. The gap between FiscalNote's market narrative and its financial reality is a cautionary case study in the say-do gap — appropriate given the subject matter.

## SCI Analysis

FiscalNote's Government Domain coverage is comparable to Quorum's but more internationally oriented. Its CQ congressional intelligence product is particularly strong for organizations monitoring federal legislative and regulatory developments. Like Quorum, it provides a sophisticated implementation of Government Domain stakeholder intelligence without architectural ambition beyond that domain.

The platform's philosophical limitation is the same as Quorum's: it is domain-specific intelligence infrastructure, not cross-domain governance architecture. FiscalNote's relationship management tools are built for government stakeholders; they have no conceptual extension to the Market, Information, or Civil Society domains. The "stakeholders" in FiscalNote's vocabulary are legislators, staffers, and regulators — a precise subset of an organization's full stakeholder ecosystem.

FiscalNote's financial difficulties are interesting from an SCI design perspective: they reflect a market that values functional legislative intelligence but has not yet articulated (or been offered) a value proposition rooted in the governance of the full stakeholder ecosystem. The premium pricing that FiscalNote attempted to sustain suggests a market willing to pay for quality intelligence, but skeptical of architectural promises not backed by demonstrated outcomes.

## Competitive Intelligence

FiscalNote's difficulties have created market uncertainty in the Government Domain that Quorum is capturing. For SCI's design partner search, FiscalNote's enterprise customers — particularly multinational corporations needing international policy intelligence — are a target segment. These organizations have already invested in sophisticated government affairs infrastructure; they understand the ROI of policy intelligence. The question is whether they are ready to think about cross-domain stakeholder governance as the next architectural layer.

FiscalNote's VoterVoice product (digital advocacy) is worth monitoring separately — it serves a segment of organizations (associations, nonprofits, advocacy groups) that are also Civil Society domain actors, creating an interesting overlap between Government and Civil Society domain management.

## Notes

FiscalNote rebranded its core platform to PolicyNote in 2024, likely to create clearer product separation from the troubled parent company brand. The CQ brand (Congressional Quarterly, a legacy congressional intelligence service) remains valuable and was preserved. Watch whether FiscalNote's financial restructuring leads to asset sales — the CQ intelligence assets, if separated, could be particularly interesting for an acquirer seeking Government Domain data depth.
