# Onclusive

**Category:** Crisis / Risk Intelligence — PR Analytics & Earned Media Measurement
**URL:** https://onclusive.com
**Scale:** London and San Francisco-headquartered; formed from the merger of AirPR and Kantar Media Monitoring (2021); rebranded from PRgloo/AirPR; serves 4,000+ companies globally; focuses on enterprise PR analytics and earned media measurement.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★★

---

## What It Does

Onclusive positions itself as the leading PR analytics platform — specifically addressing the problem that has long bedeviled communications professionals: measuring the value and impact of earned media. Its core capabilities include media monitoring (online, print, broadcast, social), earned media measurement (moving beyond clip counts toward business impact metrics), journalist and outlet intelligence, and crisis monitoring. The platform's "Analyst" product is designed for senior communications leaders who need to justify PR investment to the C-suite in financial terms.

The architectural claim is measurement rigor: Onclusive has invested in proprietary measurement methodologies (the "Onclusive Analytics" framework) that attempt to connect earned media coverage to business outcomes — website traffic, search visibility, and ultimately commercial performance. This is the most serious attempt in the Information Domain to solve the ROI measurement problem that communications leaders cite as their most persistent challenge.

A key recent product direction is the integration of social and earned media monitoring — recognizing that contemporary media stories originate and spread across both editorial channels (earned media) and social channels, requiring unified monitoring rather than separate tools.

## SCI Analysis

Onclusive occupies an interesting middle position in the Information Domain: it is more analytically sophisticated than basic monitoring platforms (Agility, Prowly) but less focused on journalist relationship management than Muck Rack. Its emphasis on measurement and business impact represents a genuine attempt to connect PR activity to organizational outcomes — which is, in embryonic form, the governance cycle closing mechanism (demonstrating what engagement produced).

The platform's philosophical limitation is that measurement of impact is not the same as governance of relationships. Onclusive tells you what your earned media coverage achieved in terms of audience reach and business metrics; it does not tell you who the journalists are as stakeholders, what posture they hold toward the organization, or how to govern the engagement systematically over time. The measurement is backward-looking; the governance question is forward-looking and relational.

From an SCI perspective, Onclusive's measurement ambition is valuable and necessary — but it is one component of the governance cycle, not the cycle itself. A platform that only measures outcomes is like an athlete who only tracks performance statistics without coaching, training, or strategic competition planning. The numbers are real; what they're missing is the architecture that produces better numbers over time.

## Competitive Intelligence

Onclusive's customer base — PR teams and CCO-level functions at organizations serious about demonstrating communications ROI — is directly relevant to SCI's positioning. These are professionals actively wrestling with the question of how to translate stakeholder engagement into demonstrable organizational value, which is precisely the governance cycle question. The frustration pattern for Onclusive customers is typically: "We can measure what happened, but we can't use that measurement to structure better decisions about who to engage and how."

The design partner from Onclusive's customer base: a VP or Director of Communications Analytics or Measurement at a large organization who has built measurement infrastructure and is being asked to make it more strategic — to go from "how much coverage did we get?" to "are we building the right stakeholder relationships for long-term organizational resilience?"

## Notes

Onclusive's formation from multiple mergers and acquisitions (AirPR + PRgloo + Kantar Media Monitoring elements) has created integration complexity; the platform is still consolidating its various product lines. The company has been refining its enterprise offering and focusing on the senior communications leader market. The explicit claim to integrate social and earned media monitoring — recognizing that the boundary between them has collapsed — is a sound architectural observation with implications beyond measurement into stakeholder signal detection.
