# Irwin

**Category:** Market Domain — Investor Relations CRM
**URL:** https://www.getirwin.com
**Scale:** Growth-stage; Canadian-headquartered (Toronto); raised C$20M Series B (2022); focused on small- to mid-cap public companies; FactSet integration for data connectivity; positioned as the IR CRM specialist vs. Q4's broader IR Ops platform.
**Relevance to SCI:** ★

---

## What It Does

Irwin is a specialist IR CRM — an investor relationship management platform built specifically for tracking, managing, and reporting on investor interactions. Its core architecture centers on investor targeting (identifying investors likely to be interested in the company's story), a purpose-built CRM for logging investor meetings and interactions, ownership analytics (tracking shareholder base changes), and reporting tools for keeping the board and C-suite informed about IR program performance.

Irwin's differentiation within the Market Domain is its CRM depth: where Q4 competes on breadth (covering the full IR Ops workflow), Irwin competes on CRM quality. The investor interaction logging, meeting preparation tools, roadshow itinerary management, and board reporting features are purpose-built for IR professionals who need to track complex, long-cycle relationships with institutional investors. The FactSet integration brings premium financial data into the CRM context.

## SCI Analysis

Irwin is the Market Domain analog of what Muck Rack is in the Information Domain — a platform that takes the relationship management dimension of stakeholder engagement seriously, within a single domain. Its emphasis on tracking interactions, maintaining context across meetings, and reporting on relationship quality are all directionally aligned with SCI's governance cycle concept applied to investor relationships.

The limitation is the same as all domain-specialist platforms: sophisticated within its domain, invisible to the others. Irwin's investors are economic stakeholders in the Market Domain; the company's regulatory posture, media reputation, and community relationships — all of which materially affect investor confidence — are structurally absent. An investor relations professional using Irwin to prepare for a meeting with a major institutional investor has no tool to integrate what that investor's proxy advisory firm is saying about the company's governance, or what a recent investigative media story might signal about investor sentiment.

## Competitive Intelligence

Irwin's customer profile — IR professionals at small- to mid-cap public companies — is a secondary priority for SCI's design partner search. These organizations have real stakeholder governance needs, but they are less likely to have the organizational complexity and budget to be early adopters of a cross-domain governance platform. Irwin customers at the larger end of the mid-cap range, however — companies with active media relations, government affairs exposure, and community stakeholder complexity — may represent interesting design partner candidates.

## Notes

The FactSet-Irwin integration is strategically significant: it connects the CRM layer with the premium financial data infrastructure that institutional investors use. This is the Market Domain's version of connecting stakeholder intelligence with external data — a model SCI would need to replicate across all four domains (legislative intelligence for Government, media monitoring for Information, community and ESG data for Civil Society).
