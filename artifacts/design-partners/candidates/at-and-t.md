## Why They're a Candidate

AT&T sponsored the Morning Breaks at the 2025 Page Annual Conference and Daniel Neal, Director of Insights and Intelligence, was a featured speaker on the data-driven communications measurement session — indicating active investment in communications intelligence infrastructure. AT&T faces all four SCI domains: government (FCC regulation, spectrum policy, FISA/national security, state PUC oversight), information (media relations for a massive consumer brand), civil society (digital equity, rural broadband, union relations), and market (heavy debt load, activist investor attention, ESG pressure on infrastructure). As the Title Sponsor's conference neighbor and a Page Society member, they're embedded in the senior CCO community.

## SCI Fit

AT&T's SCI fit centers on the Government + Market domain intersection with Information as the amplifier: telecommunications companies exist within an extraordinarily complex regulatory environment where FCC decisions, spectrum auctions, and merger reviews create simultaneous government, investor, and media stakeholder dynamics. The design partner conversation would focus on how SCI's unified stakeholder intelligence picture would change AT&T's approach to regulatory proceedings — where government relations, media communications, and investor relations currently operate in silos.

## Notes

AT&T is very large (Fortune 10 territory) — this is the primary risk factor for piloting. However, Daniel Neal's role in "Insights and Intelligence" suggests a function that is specifically positioned to evaluate and adopt new intelligence tools. The design partner conversation should be scoped to a specific business unit or regulatory context rather than enterprise-wide, to avoid procurement friction. AT&T's recent history includes the DirecTV divestiture, Warner Media spin-off, and ongoing 5G build — all multi-domain stakeholder governance challenges.
