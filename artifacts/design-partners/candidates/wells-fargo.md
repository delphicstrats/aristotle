## Why They're a Candidate

Beth Richek, Senior Vice President and Head of Corporate Affairs Communications at Wells Fargo, was identified via LinkedIn search. Wells Fargo has lived through one of the most severe multi-domain stakeholder crises in recent U.S. corporate history: the fake accounts scandal (2016-present) required simultaneous management of government (Congressional hearings, OCC/CFPB enforcement, Federal Reserve asset cap), information (sustained media hostility), civil society (consumer advocacy groups, community reinvestment scrutiny), and market (institutional investor pressure, credit rating implications). The bank has been in active reputation recovery mode for nearly a decade — making them acutely aware of what it costs to manage these domains without coordination.

## SCI Fit

Wells Fargo's SCI fit centers on the Government + Market domain intersection, with Information and Civil Society as compounding factors. The design partner conversation would focus on how SCI's governance-cycle framework could help the Corporate Affairs function coordinate across the bank's regulatory remediation posture, investor relations narrative, and ongoing community relations work — domains that must be coordinated but currently are not unified.

## Notes

Wells Fargo remains under the Federal Reserve's asset cap (a unique regulatory punishment) — meaning their government stakeholder relationship is an existential operational constraint, not a background consideration. This creates unusually high stakes for Government domain governance. However, at 230,000 employees they are at the upper end of the size range for a nimble design partner pilot. Richek's title ("Corporate Affairs Communications") is interesting — it suggests a function that bridges government affairs and communications, which is precisely SCI's integration layer.
