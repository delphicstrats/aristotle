## Why They're a Candidate

Trans Mountain Corporation built the Trans Mountain Pipeline Expansion — one of the most politically, legally, and socially contested infrastructure projects in Canadian history. Their documented Borealis implementation is not routine: they reported cutting stakeholder contact time by 70%, reporting time by 80%, and tripling data management capacity. This level of investment signals genuine institutional commitment to stakeholder governance at scale. The pipeline crosses hundreds of First Nations territories and runs through major urban centers — Civil Society and Government domain complexity at maximum intensity. They also face continuous market and media scrutiny.

## Notes

The pipeline expansion faced decades of legal challenge, political controversy, Indigenous rights litigation, and environmental opposition. Trans Mountain's team has lived multi-domain stakeholder crisis at the highest intensity. They would immediately understand SCI's value proposition — this is an organization that knows the cost of siloed stakeholder intelligence. Worth noting that Trans Mountain is Crown-owned (Government of Canada), which makes their governance requirements unusually rigorous.
