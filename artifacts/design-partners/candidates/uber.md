## Why They're a Candidate

Uber is a documented Quorum customer using the platform to build stronger stakeholder relationships in their government affairs function. Uber's multi-domain stakeholder complexity is unique among technology companies: simultaneous government pressure (taxi regulations, labor law, gig worker classification legislation across 70+ countries), civil society (driver advocacy groups, city community impact), information (media narrative on labor practices, safety, market power), and market (institutional investors evaluating regulatory risk as a material factor). Uber's government affairs function is one of the most sophisticated in technology — they have pioneered real-time legislative tracking as a core competency.

## SCI Fit

Uber's SCI fit is strongest in the Government + Civil Society domain intersection: driver classification legislation (AB5 in California, similar battles globally) requires simultaneous management of legislators, regulators, driver advocacy organizations, media, and institutional investors — all of whom have interdependent positions that shift as the legislative environment changes. SCI would provide the cross-domain governance layer that their current Quorum implementation (Government only) cannot.
