## Why They're a Candidate

Southwest Airlines is the Title Sponsor of the 2025 Page Annual Conference, indicating deep institutional commitment to senior communications practice. Whitney Eichinger, SVP and CCO, both moderated a major session ("Resiliency out of Permacrisis") and served as a speaker — demonstrating active thought leadership, not passive membership. Linda Rutherford, former CCO and now Advisor, served as 2025 Conference Chair. Southwest has faced extraordinary multi-domain stakeholder pressure since its December 2022 operational meltdown: government (DOT investigation, congressional hearings, regulatory fines), information (sustained negative media narrative and social media crisis), civil society (customer advocacy groups, labor union relations), and market (activist investor Elliott Management's 2024 campaign to replace leadership). This is the full SCI scenario.

## SCI Fit

Southwest is an ideal design partner for stress-testing SCI's full four-domain integration under real crisis conditions. The design partner conversation would focus on how unified stakeholder intelligence could have changed the response to the Elliott activist campaign — where government affairs, investor relations, media communications, and employee/customer relations were all simultaneously in play and managed by separate functions with incomplete visibility into each other's positions.

## Notes

Elliott Management's 2024-2025 activist campaign against Southwest is a textbook SCI use case: an institutional investor (Market domain) simultaneously coordinated with media (Information domain), lobbied operationally inclined board allies (Government domain), and used customer sentiment data (Civil Society domain) as leverage. Southwest's communications function navigated this without a unified governance tool. They would recognize SCI's value proposition immediately.
