## Why They're a Candidate

DTE Energy has a documented, award-level Quorum implementation for community and stakeholder engagement. Alissa Sevrioukova publicly shared their transformation story — moving from siloed, ad-hoc engagement to a data-driven, unified stakeholder model with annual "universe mapping" reviewed quarterly. This is textbook SCI-ready thinking: they've already internalized the idea that stakeholder engagement is a governance discipline, not a communications task. As a regulated utility, DTE also faces all four SCI domains simultaneously: regulatory/legislative (Government), community trust (Civil Society), media narrative (Information), and investor/ESG scrutiny (Market).

## SCI Fit

DTE is a perfect Borealis-to-SCI bridge case: they've operationalized community engagement but manage government affairs and investor relations separately. The design partner conversation would focus on unifying the stakeholder universe across Government, Civil Society, and Market into a single governance cycle — and surfacing cross-domain signal (e.g., how community trust impacts regulatory proceedings).

## Notes

Sevrioukova described their model as explicitly cross-functional: "Teams across the company from government affairs to environmental compliance contributed names, contacts, and context." This framing — stakeholder governance as an enterprise function, not a departmental one — is exactly the intellectual foundation SCI builds on.
