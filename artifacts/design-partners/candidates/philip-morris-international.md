## Why They're a Candidate

Travis Parman, Chief Communications Officer for Philip Morris International (U.S.), was a featured speaker and moderator at the 2025 Page Annual Conference — indicating active engagement with the senior CCO community and forward-thinking posture on multi-stakeholder communications. PMI is arguably the highest-complexity multi-domain stakeholder environment in global business: navigating simultaneous government regulation (across 180 markets), information/media hostility, civil society opposition, and market/investor ESG pressure during a declared corporate transformation from combustible tobacco to smoke-free products. The "transformation" narrative itself is a multi-domain stakeholder governance challenge with no clean tool to manage it.

## SCI Fit

PMI's SCI fit spans all four domains simultaneously. The design partner conversation would focus on how SCI could coordinate stakeholder intelligence across governments (regulation, lobbying, harm reduction policy), civil society (health advocacy organizations, NGOs), information (media strategy for a contested transformation narrative), and market (institutional investors evaluating ESG commitments). No existing tool currently unifies these.

## Notes

PMI's declared strategy — becoming a "smoke-free company" — requires persuading all four stakeholder domains simultaneously: regulators must approve reduced-risk products, media must cover the transformation credibly, civil society organizations must accept or engage with the harm-reduction premise, and investors must believe the transition economics. This is SCI's exact use case made manifest. PMI is large but not Fortune 10, and their Communications function has both the budget and the intellectual appetite for this kind of platform.
