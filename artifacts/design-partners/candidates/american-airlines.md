**Priority:** High

## Why They're a Candidate

Ron DeFeo, Executive Vice President of Communications and Marketing and CCO, was a featured speaker at the 2025 Page Annual Conference on the "Resiliency out of Permacrisis" panel — describing how to sustain resilience across overlapping crises. Airlines face all four SCI domains at constant intensity: government (FAA, DOT, congressional relations, airport authorities), information (consumer media, social media crises, safety narrative), civil society (labor unions, community impact, environmental groups), and market (institutional investors, debt markets, ESG ratings). American Airlines in particular has navigated consecutive crises including safety scrutiny, DEI policy controversies, labor disputes, and competitive investor pressure.

## SCI Fit

American's SCI fit centers on Government + Information + Market domain integration: how regulatory risk (FAA oversight, congressional hearings) translates into media narrative, which translates into investor confidence and credit ratings — and how the communications function can govern those interdependencies proactively rather than reactively. The design partner conversation would focus on crisis preparedness and cross-domain signal integration.

## Notes

The aviation industry is a natural SCI target: no other sector has regulators, journalists, consumer advocates, and capital markets all scrutinizing the same safety and operational decisions simultaneously. Ron DeFeo has been named in recent media coverage around American's communications challenges. Southwest Airlines CCO Whitney Eichinger was also a Page Society speaker and conference chair — both companies are in active multi-stakeholder navigation mode.
