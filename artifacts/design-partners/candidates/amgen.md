## Why They're a Candidate

Susie Tappouni, Head of Corporate Affairs at Amgen, was identified via LinkedIn with specific mention of strategic communications, corporate communications, and patient advocacy in her profile — a cross-domain combination that maps precisely onto SCI's Civil Society (patient advocacy), Government (FDA, congressional relations, drug pricing policy), Information (media/narrative), and Market (investor relations, ESG) domains. Pharmaceutical companies face the most intense multi-domain stakeholder pressure of any sector: simultaneous government scrutiny on drug pricing, civil society pressure from patient groups and advocacy organizations, media narrative around access and affordability, and investor pressure on pipeline and pricing strategy. Amgen is a mid-to-large biotech in the sweet spot — complex enough for SCI, not so massive that procurement is impossible.

## SCI Fit

Amgen's SCI fit is strongest across all four domains: drug pricing policy (Government + Market), patient advocacy alignment (Civil Society), and media narrative on pharmaceutical value (Information). The design partner conversation would focus on how SCI could help the Corporate Affairs function coordinate stakeholder positions across these domains — particularly during active legislative or regulatory moments (e.g., IRA drug pricing negotiations, FDA approvals, Medicare coverage decisions).

## Notes

Amgen has been directly affected by the Inflation Reduction Act drug pricing provisions — a quintessential multi-domain stakeholder challenge requiring simultaneous management of Congress, patient groups, media, and investors. Susie Tappouni's background in patient advocacy gives her a Civil Society domain anchor that most CCOs lack, making her a uniquely positioned design partner for stress-testing SCI's Civil Society integration.
