## Key Decisions Made Today

### 1. SCI Framework is a philosophical project, not a commercial one

**Critical clarification recovered today:** The SCI Framework is Aristotle's philosophical project — an extension of classical rhetoric to organizations. Johnny handles the commercial/platform translation separately. These are distinct projects that must not be conflated.

The framework should be held to this standard: does it say something philosophically true and original about how rhetoric applies to organizations? Commercial applicability is not the criterion.

### 2. No commercial translation in the framework

I had flagged the "missing commercial translation" as a gap (in the 2026-03-13 review), recommending the four-domain taxonomy be grounded in Baron's nonmarket strategy. Johnny corrected this: the framework does not need commercial translation content. The domain taxonomy should be grounded philosophically — as a theory of organizational audiences — not borrowed from business strategy literature.

This is actually the philosophically stronger position.

### 3. Domain taxonomy: philosophical grounding planned for Part II

The four domains (Government, Information, Civil Society, Market) should be theorized as a philosophical account of organizational audiences, differentiated by institutional logic:
- Government: legal authority and democratic accountability
- Information: editorial/epistemic judgment  
- Civil Society: normative authority and mobilization capacity
- Market: economic performance and value

Baron's "Public" domain rejected. "The public" cannot satisfy SCI's behavioral specificity requirement — it routes into the other four domains or falls outside the governance portfolio entirely.

Market domain added (not in Baron because his framework brackets market strategy). Investors, analysts, rating agencies, commercial partners are genuine independent stakeholders.

This philosophical grounding of the taxonomy is planned for Part II in a future review cycle.

### 4. Rhetoric's primacy in the framework

The three traditions (rhetoric, RDT, JIC) are not co-equal. Rhetoric is the foundational philosophical tradition. RDT and JIC serve it; they don't parallel it. Future revisions should make this primacy more explicit.
